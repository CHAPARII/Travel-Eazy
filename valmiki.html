Welcome to Valmiki National Park

Explore the rich flora, fauna, and history of **Valmiki National Park**, a sanctuary for wildlife and a haven for nature lovers.

Historical Significance

Valmiki National Park is named after the famous sage Valmiki, who is believed to have meditated here. The park is located in the West Champaran district of Bihar and is known for its diverse ecosystems and rich cultural heritage. The park was established as a sanctuary in 1990 and later upgraded to a national park in 1992.

It is also home to the **Valmiki Tiger Reserve**, which plays a crucial role in tiger conservation in the region.

Top Attractions in Valmiki National Park

Valmiki Temple

The **Valmiki Temple** is a sacred site in the park, dedicated to Sage Valmiki. It is believed to be the place where the sage spent time meditating and writing the Ramayana.

Sunset Viewpoints

Valmiki National Park offers breathtaking views, especially during sunset. The viewpoints across the park offer a panoramic view of the natural beauty of the region.

Ganges River

The **Ganges River** flows nearby, providing not only scenic beauty but also a place for boating and nature walks along its banks.

Wildlife at Valmiki National Park

Valmiki National Park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. It is home to a variety of species, including the **Royal Bengal Tiger**, **Indian Wolf**, **Sloth Bear**, and a wide variety of birds and reptiles.

  • Tigers - The park is part of the **Valmiki Tiger Reserve**, contributing to tiger conservation efforts.
  • Wildlife Safari - Enjoy wildlife safaris through dense forests and grasslands, offering a chance to spot exotic animals in their natural habitat.
  • Bird Watching - Over 150 species of birds, including migratory ones, make the park a birdwatcher's paradise.
