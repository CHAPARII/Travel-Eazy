Welcome to Dharbhanga Darbar

Discover the majestic **Dharbhanga Darbar**, a historic royal residence showcasing grandeur, culture, and architectural brilliance.

History of Dharbhanga Darbar

The **Dharbhanga Darbar** was the royal palace of the **Maharaja of Darbhanga**, built during the 18th century in the city of **Dharbhanga**, Bihar. It reflects the grandeur of the princely state of Darbhanga, which was one of the richest and most influential in the region. The palace served as the residence of the royal family, and its architectural style blends Mughal and colonial influences.

Today, the palace stands as a testament to the opulence of the past and offers a glimpse into the life of the royals who lived here. The Darbar is famous for its intricate woodwork, sprawling courtyards, and cultural significance in Bihar’s history.

Top Attractions at Dharbhanga Darbar

Royal Architecture

The **Dharbhanga Darbar** is known for its majestic architecture, which combines **Mughal**, **Rajput**, and **Colonial** styles. The intricate carvings and majestic arches are testimony to the royal aesthetics of that era.

Royal Gardens and Courtyards

The palace grounds include beautifully maintained gardens and courtyards. These serene spaces were once used for royal gatherings and festivities.

Cultural Heritage

Dharbhanga Darbar played a vital role in promoting local culture. The palace hosted many cultural events, music, and dance performances, leaving behind a rich legacy of art and traditions in the region.
